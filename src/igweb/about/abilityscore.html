Choosing Ability Scores

You can choose your ability scores in one of the following ways:

  • Choose from one of the following sets of scores, putting the high score in your most important attribute and working down:

    • 18,14,11,10,10,8
    • 16,16,12,11,11,8
    • 16,14,14,11,10,10
    • 16,14,13,12,11,10

  • Buy your scores with a point buy. Unless your DM says otherwise, you must spend exactly 32 points. The cost of each score is shown on the table below:

    ScoreCostScoreCost
    9 1 14 7
    10 2 15 9
    11 3 16 11
    12 4 17 14
    13 5 18 18

    A score of 8 or lower costs no points. You cannot buy a score higher than an 18.

  • Roll 4d6 for each score and take the highest three values. Assign the scores as you see fit. Note that this may not actually generate a playable character; if you're going to use this method be sure to clear it with your DM.

At 4th, 8th, 14th, 18th, 24th, and 28th levels you get to choose two ability scores to improve. Each ability score goes up by one point.
